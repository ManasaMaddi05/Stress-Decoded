Understanding Exam Stress

Physiological Impact

Our research captures real-time heart rate, electrodermal activity, and movement data from students during exams, revealing how stress manifests physically. Data was collected using FDA-approved Empatica E4 wristbands during midterm and final exams.

Performance Correlation

Discover the relationship between stress levels and exam performance. Our dataset bridges the gap between laboratory stress studies and real-world cognitive stressors experienced by college students during high-pressure academic situations.

Stress Management

Explore how different coping mechanisms affect physiological responses and recovery times. Our dataset includes electrodermal activity, heart rate, blood volume pulse, skin temperature, and accelerometer data from 10 students across three exam sessions.

Data Visualization

Experience interactive visualizations that bring stress data to life. Our study recorded data during 1.5-hour midterms and 3-hour finals, making complex physiological patterns accessible and meaningful for understanding real-world stress responses.

About the Dataset

This project uses a unique dataset that bridges the gap between laboratory stress studies and real-world cognitive stressors. While many experiments use artificial stimuli like electric shocks or loud noises, our data captures how people's physiological responses fluctuate during actual exam experiences.

The dataset contains recordings from 10 participants (2 female, 8 male) during three exam sessions (midterm 1, midterm 2, and finals), measuring electrodermal activity, heart rate, blood volume pulse, skin temperature, inter-beat interval, and movement data, along with corresponding grades.
